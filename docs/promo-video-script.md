# Operator Promo Video — 90s Infomercial Script

*Target: remote tech worker. Aha: meeting-tail post-work avalanche dissolved by Operator doing it all in-meeting.*

---

## Setting & Aesthetic

Everything *visual* is 90s. Everything *spoken* is modern.

- **Hero ("Johnny"):** 90s haircut (curtains / center-part), 90s clothes (oversized polo, pleated khakis or baggy jeans), 90s way of speaking ("aw geez," "you betcha" cadence).
- **Set:** beige CRT monitor, mechanical keyboard, ball mouse, mousepad with a swirl pattern. Dim room, blinds half-closed, single desk lamp. Possibly a Trapper Keeper.
- **Screen content:** modern apps (Google Meet, Slack, Linear, GitHub, Gmail) — but seen from a distance / through a CRT-curvature filter. We *don't* read the UI clearly. The hero's spoken lines tell us what's on the screen. The mix of vintage tactile world + modern software is the entire visual joke.

---

## 30-Second Script

### [0:00–0:05] COLD OPEN — END OF MEETING (slow push-in from behind)

Slow dolly push-in from behind Johnny's chair. Over his shoulder we see a Google Meet grid on a CRT — four or five teammate tiles, a startup-y mix. Audio overlap of sign-offs:

> **TEAMMATE 1:** "Cool, see you at tomorrow's standup!"
> **TEAMMATE 2:** "Let's get these issues resolved by Friday."
> **TEAMMATE 3:** "Bye!"
> **TEAMMATE 4:** "Bye guys."

Tiles wink out one by one. Johnny clicks "Leave call." He clicks his pen — *click-click* — and exhales.

### [0:05–0:13] THE LIST + THE FORGOTTEN THING

Johnny swivels slightly, pen-clicking, talking to no one in particular.

> **JOHNNY:** "Okay… so I gotta file four tickets in Linear, open that GitHub issue, merge Priya's PR, schedule the follow-up call, dig through the API docs to see if SSO callbacks are even a thing, find that auth doohickey we built six months ago that does basically the same deal…"

Beat. Pen-click stops. He squints.

> **JOHNNY:** "…and there was one more thing. What was the — was it another issue? Or the follow-up time? Did Priya say Tuesday or Thursday? Aw, geez."

Small, contained moment of frustration — pinches bridge of nose, exhales again. Not dramatic.

> **JOHNNY:** "Gee… if only there were a better way."

### [0:13–0:14] TONE FLIP

Lights warm. A triumphant 90s synth horn stinger. The CRT flickers.

### [0:14–0:18] NARRATOR ARRIVES (omniscient → direct)

A booming, godlike voice from nowhere — at first, *about* Johnny, not to him:

> **NARRATOR:** "Little did Johnny know… there *was* a better way."

Johnny freezes. Looks up, looks around. Looks at the camera.

> **JOHNNY:** "…really?"
> **NARRATOR:** "You betcha!"

Triumphant pitch:

> **NARRATOR:** "Introducing — **OPERATOR!** The AI teammate that does the work *while you're still in the meeting!*"

### [0:18–0:27] CAPABILITY MONTAGE (narrator hijacks Johnny's CRT)

Each beat = (1) narrator callout in voiceover, (2) cut to Johnny's CRT showing the actual product doing the thing — real screen recording with a 90s VHS/CRT filter (scanlines, slight chromatic aberration, soft glow), (3) quick cut to Johnny's reaction watching the screen. STARBURST callouts in Comic Sans–adjacent font.

- **"FILES TICKETS!"** → CRT: chat message in Meet → Linear ticket pops in with link → Johnny: jaw drops.
- **"OPENS GITHUB ISSUES!"** → CRT: GH issue materializes mid-meeting → Johnny: leans forward, eyes widen.
- **"READS YOUR DOCS!"** → CRT: bot answers "yes, SSO callbacks are supported" with a doc link → Johnny: nods, impressed.
- **"KNOWS YOUR CODEBASE!"** → CRT: bot says "you built this in `auth/sso_handler.py` last November" → Johnny: stands up halfway out of chair.
- **"SCHEDULES FOLLOW-UPS!"** *(cut first if pacing tight)* → CRT: calendar invite materializes → Johnny: clutches pen to chest.

> **NARRATOR:** "It uses YOUR Claude Code skills! YOUR MCP servers! YOUR model! It's like having your whole team in every meeting!"

### [0:27–0:30] CTA

Big chunky 90s lower-third, primary-color background, possibly with a rotating 3D WordArt logo:

> **OPERATOR**
> *Meetings that produce artifacts, not just words.*
> **1-800-OPERATOR.com**
> *Install free. Open source. BYO-API-key.*

Jingle voice sings: *"One-eight-hundred… **OP-ER-A-TOR-DOT-COM!**"*

Final shot: Johnny gives a thumbs-up to camera, frozen mid-grin.
